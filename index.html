Executive Summary

This report summarises the historical trajectory of dark web activity from early anonymity projects to the modern fragmented ecosystem. High-level findings: peak activity in mid-2010s, decline in centralized markets due to law enforcement and analytics, and continued adaptation by malicious actors.

Key Findings
  • Tor and similar networks enabled both privacy-preserving use and criminal markets.
  • Major takedowns (e.g., Silk Road) reduced central marketplaces but led to fragmentation.
  • Cryptocurrency enabled transactions; chain analysis has reduced some anonymity.
  • Emerging trends: increased AI use, decentralized protocols, and encrypted messaging as alternative channels.
Mitigations & Advice
  1. Use unique passwords and a password manager.
  2. Enable strong 2FA (hardware token recommended).
  3. Monitor accounts with breach services (haveibeenpwned) and enable dark web alerts.
  4. Be cautious with KYC data; avoid oversharing personal identifiers publicly.
